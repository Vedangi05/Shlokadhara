ShlokaDhara: A Multi-Speaker Sanskrit Speech Corpus for Learning Meter-Aware Prosody in Text-to-Recitation

Abstract. Sanskrit verse recitation follows Chandas, a prosodic framework defined by rhythmic patterns of Laghu (light) and Guru (heavy) syllables. Existing Sanskrit speech datasets primarily support ASR or conventional TTS and do not explicitly model the metrical structure required for verse recitation. We introduce ShlokaDhara, a 43+ hour multi-speaker Sanskrit speech corpus with diverse Chandas, recitation styles, and melodic variations. We further propose Text-to-Recite, a synthesis task that extends TTS by emphasizing metrical fidelity and prosodic correctness. Using ShlokaDhara, we benchmark several state-of-the-art multilingual TTS models. Despite the inherent complexity of Sanskrit verse recitation, XTTS-V2 achieves the best perceptual quality with a MOS of 4.01, indicating strong but still imperfect synthesis quality for this challenging task. We further conduct a human evaluation of the Text-to-Recite task with 16 Sanskrit experts. Our work establishes a benchmark for prosody-aware Sanskrit speech synthesis and computational Sanskrit recitation.
