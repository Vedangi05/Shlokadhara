ShlokaDhara: A Multi-Speaker Sanskrit Speech Corpus for Learning Meter-Aware Prosody in Text-to-Recitation

Abstract. Sanskrit verse recitation follows Chandas, a prosodic framework defined by rhythmic patterns of Laghu (light) and Guru (heavy) syllables. Existing Sanskrit speech datasets primarily support Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) or conventional Text-to-Speech (TTS) and do not explicitly model the metrical structure required for verse recitation. We introduce ShlokaDhara, a multi-speaker Sanskrit speech corpus for metrically-aware speech synthesis, containing over 43 hours of high-quality recitations from 19 speakers across diverse literary and devotional sources. The corpus spans multiple Chandas, recitation styles, and melodic variations. We further propose Text-to-Recite, a synthesis task that extends conventional TTS by emphasizing metrical fidelity, rhythmic consistency, and prosodic correctness. Using ShlokaDhara, we benchmark several state-of-the-art multilingual TTS systems. Despite the inherent complexity of Sanskrit verse recitation, which requires accurate pronunciation, Sandhi preservation, and adherence to metrical structure, XTTS-V2 achieves the best perceptual quality with a MOS of 4.01, indicating strong but still imperfect synthesis quality for this challenging task. We further conduct a human evaluation of the Text-to-Recite task with 16 Sanskrit experts, assessing pronunciation accuracy, Sandhi handling, and overall recitation quality. Our work establishes a benchmark for prosody-aware Sanskrit speech synthesis and computational Sanskrit recitation.
